## Tweet 6 — FOLLOW-UP: How to stress test your portfolio — 2 methods (PUBLICAR EN 2-3 DÍAS)

Promised I'd share how we stress test our portfolio. Here are the 2 methods we use — from quick & dirty to rigorous 🧵

**METHOD 1: The 5-minute heuristic** (spreadsheet, no code needed)

For each position, estimate 3 things:
- Rate sensitivity: LOW (-3%), MED (-7%), HIGH (-12%)
- Oil/commodity exposure: NONE (0%), LOW (-3%), HIGH (-15%)
- Recession beta: 0.5 (defensive) to 1.2 (cyclical)

Multiply all 3, weight by position size, sum. Done.

Our result: -7.3%. Quick, intuitive, good for a gut check. BUT — and this is important — it UNDERSTATES tail risk. The betas are guesses, not data. Correlations are assumed stable. In a real crisis, they're not.

⚠️ This method tells you "am I roughly OK?" It does NOT tell you what a real crash looks like.

**METHOD 2: The rigorous approach** (needs data, worth the effort)

1️⃣ Calculate REAL betas from historical price data (not guesses). Our portfolio beta came out 0.65 — much lower than the 0.80 we assumed.

2️⃣ Run Monte Carlo simulation (10K scenarios). Instead of ONE worst case, you get a DISTRIBUTION. Our results:
- P50 (most likely): -0.6%
- P25 (bad year): -14.9%
- P5 (1-in-20 disaster): -40.7%
- P1 (black swan): -62.7%

3️⃣ Apply REAL crisis data. We took actual 2008 sector drawdowns and applied them to our current positions. Result: -38.5% vs S&P -56.8%. We'd lose, but 18pp less than the market.

4️⃣ Check crisis correlations. Normal correlation between our positions: 0.15. In worst market days: 0.17. Barely moves — because we diversified across countries (France, UK, Netherlands, US) not just sectors.

5️⃣ Check liquidity. Can you actually SELL in a panic? Every one of our positions trades 50x-10,000x our position size daily. No liquidity trap.

**The honest conclusion?**

The quick method made us feel safe (-7.3%). The rigorous method showed us the real tail: a 1-in-20 year we lose -40%. That's uncomfortable — but KNOWING it is the whole point.

We found our most exposed position had the highest beta AND the worst fundamentals. We sold it the next morning 🔪

The best portfolio isn't the one that never loses. It's the one where you KNOW what you'll lose, and you've decided you can live with it.
